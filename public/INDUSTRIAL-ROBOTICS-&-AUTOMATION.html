INDUSTRIAL ROBOTICS & AUTOMATION

The future of technology, made now

Why Choose This Course?

Robots are becoming the standard in factories, warehouses, smart infrastructure, and even construction. This track is for learners who want to understand how automation really works—from how a robot arm moves, to how engineers troubleshoot failures. You'll build practical “automation thinking” and a strong safety mindset, so you can confidently follow how real robotic systems are designed and operated.

About this Course

Industrial Robotics & Automation is a structured pathway that takes learners from fundamentals to real-world system integration. Across three levels, you'll learn the following:


  • Robot anatomy (joints, end effectors, sensors)
  • motion concepts (paths, kinematics)
  • control logic (task sequencing, PLC-style signals)

As you progress, you'll move into reading robotic workcells, troubleshooting common failures, and finally designing a realistic automation system with integration, reliability planning, and human-robot safety culture.

Career Pathways

Graduates may be suited for roles or pathways such as but not limited to:

  • Robotics Technician (robot setup, calibration, basic programming support)
  • Automation Technician / Specialist (workcell operations, troubleshooting, maintenance support)
  • Junior Automation Engineer (workflow design, control logic, integration planning)
  • PLC / Controls Assistant (signals, interlocks, start/stop logic, diagnostics support)
  • Manufacturing / Warehouse Automation Associate (robotic cells + operational improvements)
  • Field Support / Application Engineer (help deploy and support robotic systems)
  • System Integrator (support role—assisting with connecting robots, sensors, and controllers)

Entry Requirements

Age: This module is recommended to candidates aged 16 - 25, younger participants (13 - 15) are also encouraged to join if interested.

Prior Experience: No prior experience in robotics, automation, construction, or programming is required. The track starts from fundamentals and builds up progressively.

Level 1 - Foundations of Industrial Robotics

Target: Ages 16-25 (Younger ages of 13 - 15 are also encouraged)
Total Duration: 20 hours
Delivery Timeline: 4-6 weeks
Assessment: Concept & safety quiz + mini-project

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, learners will:
  • Explain what industrial robots are and why industries use them (manufacturing, logistics, construction).
  • Identify core robot parts (axes/joints, end effectors, sensors) and describe what each does.
  • Describe how robots move in a simple, visual way (reach/workspace, basic motion types) without heavy math.
  • Read and communicate a simple robot workflow using correct terms and safe practices.
  • Demonstrate basic safety awareness (hazards, safety zones, correct behaviour around robots).

Level 2 - Applied Robotic Systems & Automation Logic

Target: Ages 13-25 & Early-Career Professionals
Total Duration: 32 hours
Delivery Timeline: 6-8 weeks
Assessment: Workflow + troubleshooting analysis

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, learners will:
  • Interpret a basic robotic workcell layout and explain how components connect (robot, tooling, sensors, signals).
  • Distinguish common motion modes (joint vs Cartesian) and explain how speed/safety trade-offs affect operation.
  • Build or modify a simple task sequence for a real robotic task (pick-and-place / process steps) using structured logic.
  • Explain PLC-style control concepts at a practical level (signals, interlocks, start/stop logic).
  • Troubleshoot common failures using a step-by-step diagnostic approach and propose reasonable fixes.

The content curated builds upon the skills of Level 1 with:

Level 3 - Advanced Automation & System Integration

Target: Ages 13-25, Serious students & Professionals
Total Duration: 42 hours
Delivery Timeline: 8-10 weeks
Assessment: System design & deployment project

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, learners will be:
  • Design a realistic robotic workcell concept with constraints (space, safety, throughput, reliability).
  • Explain how robots integrate with surrounding systems (networks, sensors/vision, tooling, control systems, monitoring).
  • Justify motion/path choices for more complex coordination scenarios (advanced motion planning concepts).
  • Create a basic preventive maintenance and reliability plan to reduce downtime and improve consistency.
  • Produce and defend a capstone system proposal clearly (what it does, how it works, safety considerations, and why the design makes sense).

The content curated builds provides students with:
  • Industry-ready experience
  • Pre-immersion qualification

Learning Format:
  • Advanced guided lessons with instruction
  • System design work
  • Review & presentation
Module 1: Designing a Robotic Workcell
Outcomes:
  • Students can design a basic workcell layout that accounts for space, safety zones, and workflow efficiency.
  • Students can identify constraints (reach, payload, cycle time, access, guarding) and explain their design impact.
  • Students can justify layout choices based on practical trade-offs (throughput vs safety vs cost).
Lesson Time: 6 hours
Activities:
  • Layout planning of a industry standard robotic workcell
  • constraint analysis different workcell layouts
Module 2: Integrating Robots with Other Systems
Outcomes:
  • Students can explain how robots connect with PLCs, sensors, vision systems, and supporting equipment.
  • Students can read and describe simple network/system diagrams at a high level (signals, data flow, control boundaries).
  • Students can identify integration risks (timing, communication failures, mismatched assumptions) and suggest mitigations.
Lesson Time: 6 hours
Activities:
  • Network & system diagrams
  • Exploring integration logic for robotic systems
Module 3: Advanced Motion, Paths, and Coordination
Outcomes:
  • Students can explain more advanced motion concepts (coordination, path planning considerations, collision awareness).
  • Students can compare coordination approaches (single robot vs multi-robot workflows) and when each is suitable.
  • Students can identify common coordination challenges (handoffs, timing conflicts, shared workspace risks).
Lesson Time: 6.5 hours
Activities:
  • Robotics motion concepts
  • Multi-robot coordination approaches & examples
Module 4: Preventive Maintenance and Reliability
Outcomes:
  • Students can describe common wear points and failure risks in robotic systems (mechanical, electrical, tooling, sensors).
  • Students can build a basic preventive maintenance plan aligned to uptime and safety goals.
  • Students can use reliability thinking to reduce downtime (inspection routines, spares planning, simple checklists).
Lesson Time: 5 hours
Activities:
  • Common areas of failure in robotics
  • Maintenance planning scenarios
Module 5: Human-Robot Collaboration and Safety Culture
Outcomes:
  • Students can explain what human-robot collaboration is and how it differs from traditional guarded cells.
  • Students can identify hazards and perform a basic risk assessment using structured scenarios.
  • Students can propose safety behaviours and procedures that support a strong safety culture on site.
Lesson Time: 5 hours
Activities:
  • identify and explain what human-robot collaborations is
  • Risk assessment exercises
Module 6: Automation in Construction & Smart Infrastructure
Outcomes:
  • Students can describe how automation is applied in construction and smart infrastructure contexts.
  • Students can compare these environments to factories (variable conditions, mobility, safety constraints, logistics).
  • Students can explain how robotics supports tasks like fabrication, inspection, and 3D printing workflows.
Lesson Time: 5 hours
Activities:
  • How automation is used in the industry
  • Walkthroughs demonstrating construction & 3D printing cases
Module 7: Capstone Project
Outcomes:
  • Students can create a realistic automation system concept and explain how it works end-to-end.
  • Students can justify design decisions clearly using constraints, safety, reliability, and operational logic.
  • Students can present their solution in a professional format (system diagram + explanation + reasoning).
Lesson Time: 3.5 hours (Guided session)
Activities:
  • Creat a realistic system design
  • Explain how the system designed works
  • Develop justifications for the system developed
