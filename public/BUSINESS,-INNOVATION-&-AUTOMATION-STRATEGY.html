BUSINESS, INNOVATION & AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Making Automation Viable, Scalable, and Ethical/p>

Why Choose This Course?

This course is for learners who want to understand what makes automation succeed in the real world. You will learn how to judge costs and returns, choose the right business model, plan market entry, and handle risks like regulation and adoption. It also covers ethics and workforce impact so decisions are responsible, not just profitable.

About this Course

You will learn the strategy side of robotics and automation, from basic economics to global scaling, then full deployment planning. The learning moves from understanding cost structures and business models, to building go to market plans and financial models, then to leadership topics like organisational change, funding pathways, governance, and execution roadmaps. By the end, you can propose a realistic automation strategy and defend it to stakeholders.

Career Pathways

Graduates may be suited for roles or pathways such as but not limited to:

  • Business analyst for automation or digital transformation
  • Strategy and operations associate
  • Innovation and partnerships executive
  • Product or program coordinator for robotics solutions
  • Market development associate for emerging technologies
  • Business development executive for automation services

Entry Requirements

Age: This module is recommended to candidates aged 18 - 35, younger participants (13 - 15) are also encouraged to join if interested.

Prior Experience: No prior experience in business, construction, or robotics is required.

Level 3 - Automation Strategy & Deployment

Target: Ages 13-30 & Advanced students & professionals
Total Duration: 38 hours
Delivery Timeline: 8-10 weeks
Assessment: Automation strategy capstone

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, learners will be:
  • Design a full automation strategy from vision to execution roadmap
  • Communicate and align with stakeholders including leadership, partners, and public sector bodies
  • Lead responsible deployment with governance, ethics, funding options, and change management

The content curated builds provides students with:
  • Industry-ready experience
  • Pre-immersion qualification

Learning Format:
  • Advanced guided lessons with instruction
  • Project development
  • Presentation & defence
Module 1: Enterprise Automation Strategy
Outcomes:
  • Students can define strategic goals and where automation fits in the enterprise
  • Students can prioritise use cases based on value, feasibility, and risk
  • Students can build a high level portfolio view of initiatives
Lesson Time: 6 hours
Activities:
  • Select one organisation type and one automation use case
  • Define the goal, scope, and success KPIs
  • Prioritise what to automate first and justify why
  • Outline required people, process, and tech changes
Module 2: Automation in Emerging Markets
Outcomes:
  • Students can identify constraints that affect viability in low resource contexts
  • Students can adapt business and deployment models to local realities
  • Students can explain trade offs among cost, impact, and maintainability
Lesson Time: 6 hours
Activities:
  • Choose one emerging market context and document key constraints
  • Adapt the solution design to fit power, water, labour, and supply chain realities
  • Identify the minimum viable pilot setup and assumptions
  • List the top risks unique to that context and mitigations
Module 3: Organizational Change & Adoption
Outcomes:
  • Students can explain why automation fails without change management
  • Students can plan adoption steps including training, roles, and incentives
  • Students can identify resistance points and how to address them
Lesson Time: 5 hours
Activities:
  • Map stakeholders and define their concerns and incentives
  • Create a training plan with timelines and learning checks
  • Draft a simple comms plan for rollout phases
  • Define adoption metrics and how you will track them
Module 4: Funding, Grants & Public/Private Models
Outcomes:
  • Students can compare funding sources such as grants, blended finance, and partnerships
  • Students can match funding approach to project type and stakeholder needs
  • Students can outline a simple funding plan for a deployment
Lesson Time: 5 hours
Activities:
  • Identify at least 2 funding sources and why they fit
  • Draft a basic stakeholder value exchange for each party
  • Outline a simple budget split and funding timeline
  • Define what evidence you need to unlock funding tranches
Module 5: Ethics, Governance & Long-Term Impact
Outcomes:
  • Students can define governance controls for safety, accountability, and transparency
  • Students can identify long term ethical risks and mitigation measures
  • Students can build guardrails that support responsible scaling
Lesson Time: 5 hours
Activities:
  • Create an ethics checklist for safety, jobs, and community impact
  • Define governance roles and decision rights
  • Set long term impact metrics and reporting cadence
  • Write a short risk escalation process for ethical issues
Module 6: Execution Roadmaps
Outcomes:
  • Students can create an execution roadmap with phases, timelines, and dependencies
  • Students can define roles, milestones, and decision checkpoints
  • Students can explain how to manage risk during rollout
Lesson Time: 5 hours
Activities:
  • Build a phased roadmap from pilot to scale
  • Assign roles, owners, and dependencies per phase
  • Create a risk register with likelihood, impact, mitigations
  • Define go no go gates between phases
Module 7: Capstone - Automation Strategy Proposal
Lesson Time: 6 hours
Activities:
Capstone Project
  • Compile a complete proposal pack across strategy, market entry, finance, governance, and roadmap
  • Prepare a stakeholder pitch deck and executive summary
  • Present and defend decisions in a panel style Q and A
  • Capture feedback and revise the plan to improve feasibility
