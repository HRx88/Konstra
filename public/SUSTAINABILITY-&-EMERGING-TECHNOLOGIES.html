SUSTAINABILITY & EMERGING TECHNOLOGIES

How to build a sustainable environment

Why Choose This Course?

This course is for learners who want to understand how technology can solve real sustainability problems, not just talk about them. You will learn how to think in systems, measure impact, and spot where automation and better design can reduce waste, emissions, and cost. It is especially useful if you care about building solutions that still work in low resource environments.

About this Course

You will explore sustainability through practical lenses like materials, circular design, automation, and impact measurement. The learning builds from fundamentals to applied redesign, then to planning solutions at scale with real constraints like policy, infrastructure, community needs, and risk. By the end, you can explain sustainability clearly and turn concepts into a structured deployment plan.

Career Pathways

Graduates may be suited for roles or pathways such as but not limited to:

  • Sustainability analyst or sustainability associate
  • ESG and impact reporting executive
  • Sustainability program coordinator for NGOs or social enterprises
  • Sustainable design or innovation associate
  • Climate tech operations or project assistant
  • Urban resilience or community development project assistant

Entry Requirements

Age: This module is recommended to candidates aged 15 - 30, younger participants (13 - 14) are also encouraged to join if interested.

Prior Experience: No prior experience in sustainability, automation, or 3D printing is required.

What You Will Learn

Total Duration: 20 hours
Delivery Timeline: 4-6 weeks
Assessment: Sustainability concepts & systems thinking test

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, learners will:
  • Explain sustainability using systems thinking
  • Identify where automation improves impact in areas like materials, energy, and waste
  • Use correct sustainability terms and interpret simple system maps and metrics

The content curated is crafted in alignment with:
  • Polytechnic short courses
  • Intro certification standards
  • Parent expectations for commitment without overload

  • Diagrams & system maps
  • Short applied exercises
