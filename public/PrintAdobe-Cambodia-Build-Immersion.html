Area of Focus

Location: Stueng Meanchey, Phnom Penh, Cambodia

Konstra's area of focus is Stueng Meanchey II in Khan Meanchey, Phnom Penh, near the former Stueng Meanchey dumpsite and Lenkov Village, where vulnerable communities live in modest housing along the canal. Even after major canal clean up works in 2023, the district still faces drainage stress during heavy rains and occasional land instability from erosion, and parts of the community remain economically fragile due to long-standing hardship tied to the former landfill area.

Itinerary

Day 1: Singapore ⌯✈︎ Phnom Penh (Arrival)

Participants will arrive in Phnom Penh and be transferred to the accommodation. The day focuses on settling in and understanding the context of the immersion. A short orientation session will be conducted to introduce the programme objectives, safety guidelines, and an overview of the community and project site. Participants will also have the opportunity to meet the Konstra team and fellow learners.

Day 2: Phnom Penh, Stueng Meanchey Community, Project Site, and Local Partner Facilities Visits

The second day focuses on on-ground exposure. Participants will visit the project area to observe existing conditions and understand the challenges faced by the local community. Guided walkthroughs and discussions will provide insight into housing, infrastructure, and environmental considerations. Learners will engage in dialogue with partners to better appreciate how design decisions are shaped by real-world constraints. Participants will take part in structured learning activities with our partners in the region, which may include observing 3D printing processes, understanding workflow planning, and discussing sustainability considerations. Time will be allocated for team discussions and reflection sessions to consolidate learning and share observations. This day emphasises translating experience into meaningful insights.

Day 3: Phnom Penh ⌯✈︎ Singapore (Return)

The final day focuses on consolidating key takeaways from the immersion. Participants will reflect on their learning journey and discuss how the experience can be applied to future academic or professional work. The programme concludes with preparation for departure and return travel.

FAQs

Who is this programme suitable for?

This programme is suitable for PrintAdobe learners and individuals who are interested in hands-on learning, community engagement, and real-world exposure. No prior overseas project experience is required, but participants should be open-minded and willing to learn.

Do I need prior construction or technical experience?

No prior construction or technical experience is required. The programme focuses on learning through observation, guided activities, and engagement rather than technical execution.

Will accommodation and meals be provided?

Yes. Accommodation and daily meals will be provided for the duration of the immersion programme to ensure participants are supported throughout the trip.

Is the programme safe?

Participant safety is a priority. Konstra works with trusted local partners and implements basic safety briefings and on-ground supervision throughout the programme.

Will there be guidance during the trip?

Yes. Participants will be guided by Konstra team members and project partners who will provide context, support, and structured learning throughout the immersion.

Is this a volunteer programme or a study tour?

This programme is a learning immersion rather than a volunteer or sightseeing tour. The focus is on exposure, understanding real-world challenges, and experiential learning.

Immersive Build Experience — Cambodia

For advanced learners, PrintAdobe offers a supervised overseas build experience in Cambodia, where theory meets real construction environments.

Participants observe and support live 3D construction printing in low-resource settings, gaining first-hand exposure to how robotics, materials, logistics, and site constraints interact in the real world. This experience deepens understanding of construction automation while developing professional awareness of deployment challenges beyond simulations.

What you will take away

This is a field immersion designed to connect learning to real constraints, real people, and real decisions.

Real world exposure

Observe how housing, infrastructure, and community systems function on the ground and how trade offs are made when resources are limited.

Human centered thinking

Learn to listen first, ask better questions, and translate lived experiences into practical needs and design considerations.

Problem framing under constraints

Practice defining the right problem before jumping to solutions, balancing safety, cost, time, and feasibility.

Collaboration and reflection

Build team communication through guided debriefs, documenting insights and turning observations into action steps.
