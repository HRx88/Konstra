3D PRINTING IN CONSTRUCTION

Explore the world of 3D Construction with Konstra

Why Choose This Course?

Learn how buildings can be printed, not assembled. This track gives you a clear, step-by-step progression from understanding how construction 3D printing works, to planning prints reliably, to learning what it takes to deploy the technology in real-world sites—especially in emerging markets where cost, speed, and sustainability matter.

About this Course

This is a 3-level pathway that takes you from fundamentals → workflows → real deployment.

  • Level 1: Level 1: Core concepts (printers, materials, process, safety) so you understand the full pipeline.
  • Level 2: Print-ready thinking (design rules, toolpaths, QC, calibration, monitoring) so prints work consistently.
  • Level 3: Site execution (logistics, local materials, regulations, partnerships, community, risk) so projects can run in real conditions.

Career Pathways

Graduates may be suited for roles or pathways such as but not limited to:

  • Construction 3D Printing Technician (Trainee)
  • Digital Fabrication Assistant / Technician
  • Robotic Construction Site Support
  • Materials & QA Assistant (Printable Mix / QC)
  • Deployment / Project Planning Assistant (Emerging Markets)

Entry Requirements

Age: This module is recommended to candidates aged 16 - 25, younger participants (13 - 15) are also encouraged to join if interested.

Prior Experience: Prior experience on construction or 3D printing is not required

What You Will Learn

Level 1 - Foundations of 3D Construction Printing

Target: Ages 16-25 (advanced 13-18)
Total Duration: 22 hours
Delivery Timeline: 4-6 weeks
Assessment: Concept & scenario based quiz + group project

Learning Outcomes

This course aims to give learners a solid mental model of how construction 3D printing works — without overwhelming them. By answering the question of:

“How does a robot actually print a building?”

Upon completion of this course, learners will:
  • Explain end-to-end how a construction printer turns a digital model into a physical structure.
  • Describe key hardware, materials, workflows, and use cases using correct terminology.
  • Be eligible for the PrintAdobe Certified Construction Printer — Level 1 priced at SGD $600

The content curated is crafted in alignment with:
  • Polytechnic short courses
  • Intro certification standards
  • Parent expectations for commitment without overload

Learning Format:
  • Pre-recorded video lessons
  • Short readings and diagrams
  • Simple case examples from Konstra projects
  • Lightweight quizzes after each module
Module 1: Why 3D Printing in Construction?
Outcomes:
  • Summarize benefits and limitations vs conventional construction (cost, speed, labor, sustainability).
  • Recognize major global pilots and emerging markets using the tech.
Activities: Short lecture + case mini-debate; news-article critique (what's hype vs reality?)
Assessment: Reflection note or short quiz.
Delivery: Classroom/online.
Module 2: Core Concepts & Vocabulary
Outcomes:
  • Define key terms (gantry, robotic arm, toolpath, rheology, buildability, layer height, G-code).
  • Sketch the “digital-to-built” pipeline at a high level.
Activities: Concept-mapping exercise; vocabulary card sort.
Assessment: Concept map submission + short MCQ.
Delivery: Online + worksheet.
Module 3: Printers, Motion & Control
Outcomes:
  • Compare different construction printer types and where each is used.
  • Identify main subsystems (motion system, pump, nozzle, control, safety).
Activities: Virtual or on-site printer walk-through; subsystem identification task.
Assessment: Labelled diagram + oral/written explanation.
Delivery: Ideally on Konstra hardware; fallback: video/simulation.
Module 4: Materials 101 - Printable Concrete & Beyond
Outcomes:
  • Describe what makes a mix pumpable and buildable.
  • List common admixtures and their roles (accelerators, superplasticizers, fibers).
Activities: Lab demo with mortar consistency comparisons; mix “cards” exercise.
Assessment: Short lab worksheet.
Delivery: Lab-based (Konstra or partner lab).
Module 5: The Printing Process & Quality Basics
Outcomes:
  • Trace a simple wall print sequence (start, perimeters, layers, stops).
  • Identify common defects (sagging, cold joints, misalignment) from photos/videos.
Activities: Watch annotated print run; group diagnosis of “what went wrong” scenarios.
Assessment: Scenario-based quiz.
Delivery: Video from Konstra projects + guided discussion.
Module 6: Use Cases, Safety & Ethics
Outcomes:
  • Match typical applications (housing, infrastructure, emergency shelters) to contexts.
  • Describe core safety considerations and ethical issues (jobs, community impact, code compliance).
Activities: Safety checklist game; mini case on housing in an emerging market.
Assessment: Group poster / slide or short written response.
Delivery: Classroom/online.
Assessment
Test Format: 30-40 item online test (concepts + scenario questions) plus a short group presentation explaining “How our robot prints a basic house wall.”​

Level 2 - Robotic Printing Workflows & Materials

Target: Ages 16-25 (advanced 13-18)
Total Duration: 33 hours
Delivery Timeline: 6-8 weeks
Assessment: Capstone mini-project

Learning Outcomes

This course aims to move from “understanding” to operational thinking. By answering the question of:

“How do we print walls, openings, and structures reliably?”

Upon completion of this course, learners will:
  • Convert a simple design into a print-ready concept respecting design rules.
  • Adjust key process and material parameters to maintain print quality.
  • Be eligible for the PrintAdobe Certified Construction Printer — Level 2 priced at SGD $900

The content curated builds upon the skills of Level 1 with:
  • Serious technical depth
  • Strong step-up from Level 1
  • Higher value → justifies higher price

Learning Format:
  • Deeper technical videos
  • Workflow diagrams and simulations
  • Material behaviour scenarios
  • Group-based case analysis (remote-friendly)
Module 1: Design for Printability
Outcomes:
  • Apply geometric rules (minimum thickness, radius, overhang limits) to a simple layout.
  • Identify features that are hard or impossible to print.
Activities: CAD-based exercise modifying a conventional wall layout into a printable one.​
Assessment: Before/after design submission with brief justification.
Delivery: CAD lab or online CAD.
Module 2: Slicing & Toolpath Basics
Outcomes:
  • Explain layer height, print speed, infill/voids, and path continuity in concrete printing.
  • Interpret a simple toolpath visualization.
Activities: Use slicing software to generate and inspect toolpaths for a small wall segment.
Assessment: Short practical quiz (identify parameters, predict outcome).
Delivery: Computer lab / simulation.
Module 3: Mix Design & Quality Control
Outcomes:
  • Outline a basic printable mix design and key QC tests (slump flow/flow table, penetration, early strength).
  • Relate changes in water or admixture dosage to print behavior.
Activities: Lab mixing demo or virtual lab; analyze QC data from real print trials.
Assessment: Lab report or data-analysis worksheet.
Delivery: Lab or remote data set from Konstra.
Module 4: Printer Setup, Calibration & Operation
Outcomes:
  • Perform or describe coordinate setup, zeroing, and path verification steps.
  • Run through a safe start-up and shutdown sequence.
Activities: On-printer or simulator practice; checklist run-through.
Assessment: Practical competency checklist + short oral/written test.
Delivery: Konstra hardware or high-fidelity simulator.
Module 5: Process Window & Environmental Effects
Outcomes:
  • Explain the relationship between speed, extrusion, and layer stability.
  • Propose parameter tweaks for hot, cold, windy, or rainy conditions.
Activities: Parameter “what-if” simulation; case study of a failed hot-climate print.
Assessment: Scenario-based assignment.
Delivery: Classroom/online.
Module 6: Automation, Sensing & Monitoring
Outcomes:
  • Describe how sensors (flow, pressure, position) and scanning (LiDAR, cameras) support quality.
  • Distinguish open-loop vs closed-loop control in simple terms.
Activities: Video/visualization of monitored print; interpret basic sensor plots.
Assessment: Short written responses or mini-quiz.
Delivery: Classroom/online.
Module 7: Finishing, Testing & Documentation
Outcomes:
  • List typical post-processing and integration steps (grouting, reinforcement tie-ins, finishes).
  • Explain basic mechanical testing and documentation requirements.
Activities: Site or lab photos walk-through; documentation template exercise.
Assessment: Sample QA/inspection report for a printed test element.
Delivery: Classroom.
Assessment
Test Format: Capstone mini-project: teams take a simple house wall from concept → print-aware design → sliced toolpaths → process parameter plan + QC checklist (graded against a rubric).

Level 3 - Site Deployment & Emerging Market Builds

Target: Ages 16-25 (advanced 13-18)
Total Duration: 45 hours
Delivery Timeline: 8-10 weeks
Assessment: Capstone Project

Learning Outcomes

This is where PrintAdobe becomes non-generic. By answering the question of:

“How do we deploy this technology in real, imperfect environments?”

Upon completion of this course, learners will be:
  • Plan a full deployment of a construction 3D printer in a constrained/emerging-market context.
  • Address logistics, regulation, community, and risk—not just technology.
  • eligible for the PrintAdobe Certified Construction Printer — Level 3 (Industry Ready) priced at SGD $1,500

The content curated builds provides students with:
  • Industry-ready experience
  • Pre-immersion qualification
  • Professional-grade certification

Learning Format:
  • Advanced case studies
  • Site deployment simulations
  • Real constraints (terrain, climate, logistics)
  • Mentor or instructor feedback
Module 1: Site Selection & Ground Conditions
Outcomes:
  • Define criteria for a viable print site in low-resource contexts.
  • Identify key tolerances and preparation steps for foundations and printer setup.
Activities: Analyze 2-3 candidate sites (photos, basic data); choose the best and justify.
Assessment: Short site selection report.
Delivery: Classroom/Online
Module 2: Logistics, Power & Water
Outcomes:
  • Map transport routes, staging areas, and storage for a remote project.
  • Propose power and water solutions under constrained infrastructure.
Activities: Logistics mapping on a simple site plan; utility planning exercise.
Assessment: One-page logistics & utilities plan.
Delivery: Online
Module 3: Local Materials & Market Realities
Outcomes:
  • Assess how local cement, sand, and aggregates might affect mix design.
  • Strategize around supply-chain reliability for materials and spare parts.
Activities: “Local materials dossier” review; adaptation brainstorming.
Assessment: Short memo on mix and supply-chain strategy.
Delivery: Lab.
Module 4: Regulations, Financing & Partnerships
Outcomes:
  • Identify key regulators, approvals, and code questions for a pilot project.
  • Sketch basic funding/partnership models with NGOs, governments, or developers.
Activities: Stakeholder-mapping workshop; simple business-case canvas.
Assessment: 2-3 page concept note (problem, partners, approvals, budget sketch).
Delivery: Classroom/Online
Module 5: Community-Centred Design & Workforce
Outcomes:
  • Integrate cultural, climatic, and social factors into printed housing layouts.
  • Outline a local training and jobs plan around the printer.
Activities: Co-design exercise using a fictional community brief; role-play with “community stakeholders.”
Assessment: Revised layout + 1-page community engagement and training plan.
Delivery: Classroom/online.
Module 6: Deployment Playbook & Risk Management
Outcomes:
  • Draft a phased deployment plan (pilot → cluster → scale-up).
  • Build a basic risk register with mitigations for technical and non-technical risks.
Activities: Group “playbook” drafting; risk-identification workshop.
Assessment: Deployment playbook (timeline, roles, risks, mitigations).
Delivery: Online.
Module 7: Capstone - Emerging Market Build Plan
Outcomes:
  • Integrate all previous modules into a coherent, realistic project proposal.
  • Communicate technical and social aspects clearly to non-experts.
Activities: Team project on a chosen country/site using Konstra as technology partner.
Assessment:
  • Integrate all previous modules into a coherent, realistic project proposal.
  • Communicate technical and social aspects clearly to non-experts.
Delivery: Classroom/online.
